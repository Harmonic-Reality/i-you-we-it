## **Completion as Tolerance Contraction**

  

Large-scale structured work fails when completion criteria are undefined.

  

Without explicit completion thresholds, iteration does not reliably converge.

  

It oscillates.

  

Completion is not emotional satisfaction.

  

It is the satisfaction of declared tolerance thresholds.

---

### **Error as Responsibility Violation**

  

Error is contextual.

  

An error is not stylistic weakness.

  

An error is a violation of declared responsibility for a Content Unit within its current lifecycle cycle.

  

For example:

  

During the scoping cycle:

  

Structural absence or overlapping responsibility constitutes error.

  

During the structuring cycle:

  

Boundary ambiguity or architectural misplacement constitutes error.

  

During the drafting cycle:

  

Behavioral drift relative to the declared lens constitutes error.

  

During the refinement cycle:

  

Unanchored abstraction or semantic looseness constitutes error.

  

During the audit cycle:

  

Terminology drift, role-token violation, or cross-sectional contradiction constitutes error.

  

Error classification depends on both unit function and lifecycle stage.

  

Responsibility defines acceptable deviation.

---

### **Tolerance as Control Variable**

  

Tolerance defines the allowable deviation for a unit at a specific stage.

  

Tolerance is inversely proportional to degrees of freedom.

  

High tolerance permits exploration.

  

Low tolerance enforces precision.

  

Tolerance must contract intentionally.

  

Without tolerance control, refinement may fail to stabilize.

  

With uncontrolled contraction, work may freeze prematurely.

  

Tolerance functions as a governing variable of convergence under constraint.

---

### **Tolerance Across Lifecycle Cycles**

  

Each lifecycle cycle reduces a specific class of allowable deviation.

  

During the scoping cycle:

  

Rough phrasing and redundancy may be tolerated.

Structural absence is not tolerated.

  

During the structuring cycle:

  

Stylistic imperfection may be tolerated.

Misplacement or boundary ambiguity is not tolerated.

  

During the drafting cycle:

  

Density variance may be tolerated.

Behavioral inconsistency is not tolerated.

  

During the refinement cycle:

  

Semantic looseness is minimized.

Abstraction without anchoring is not tolerated.

  

During the audit cycle:

  

Normalization error is not tolerated.

Terminology drift, role-token violation, and cross-sectional contradiction are not tolerated.

  

Tolerance narrows sequentially across lifecycle cycles.

  

The structural boundary remains constant.

  

Allowable deviation contracts.

---

### **Encapsulated Units and Completion**

  

Each Content Unit carries its own tolerance threshold.

  

Content Units include:

  

• Spine

• Lens

• Section

• Structural checkpoint

• Compiled structure

  

Completion occurs when a Content Unit operates within the declared tolerance band of its current lifecycle cycle.

  

Completion does not imply permanence.

  

It implies stability under Reinjection.

  

If Reinjection reintroduces the same instability, tolerance was exceeded.

  

Iteration continues.

---

### **Convergence as Controlled Contraction**

  

Refinement is not expansion.

  

It is progressive contraction of allowable deviation.

  

Artifact volume may increase.

  

Degrees of freedom decrease.

  

Convergence tends to occur when structural error classes are reduced sequentially.

  

Only one dominant error class is addressed at a time.

  

Isolation of error classes reduces cognitive load.

  

Sequential contraction increases predictability.

### **Relationship to I-You-We-It**

  

Within I-You-We-It:

  

• The I declares tolerance thresholds.

• The You executes refinement within those thresholds.

• The We accumulates stabilized corrections.

• The It reflects the current deviation band.

  

Completion occurs when The It remains stable under Reinjection within declared tolerance.
