## **Encapsulation and Interface Stability**

  

Encapsulation is the stabilization of meaning within bounded structural units.

  

It is not formatting. It is boundary enforcement.

  

Each encapsulated unit defines:

  

• Scope

• Responsibility

• Vocabulary

• Abstraction level

• Interface surface

  

Encapsulation converts undifferentiated artifact into structured components.

  

Structure reduces variance under constraint.

---

### **Encapsulation as Boundary Control**

  

Without encapsulation, an artifact behaves as a continuous surface.

  

Changes propagate unpredictably. Revisions alter adjacent meaning. Layer transitions blur.

  

Encapsulation introduces separation.

  

Separation reduces uncontrolled propagation risk.

  

Each unit absorbs modification within declared boundaries.

  

Boundary clarity functions as architectural control.

---

### **Interface Formation**

  

An encapsulated unit becomes an interface when:

  

• Its terminology remains stable

• Its responsibility is not reinterpreted

• Its boundaries are respected

• Its internal logic is self-contained

  

Interfaces are not declared symbolically.

  

They emerge through reinforcement.

  

Reinforcement stabilizes interpretation.

  

Stable interpretation permits safe reference.

  

Other units may then interact without redefining meaning.

  

This forms compound architecture.

---
