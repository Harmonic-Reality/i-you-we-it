## **Gap Detection and Probing**

  

Completion is not equivalent to stability.

  

Structured content may be coherent, dense, and internally aligned while remaining structurally fragile.

  

Gap detection and probing are late-stage convergence disciplines.

  

They verify that prior lifecycle cycles achieved their intended contraction of deviation.

  

They do not synthesize structure.

  

They test it.

---

### **Gap Detection Across Lifecycle Cycles**

  

Each lifecycle cycle introduces a dominant instability class.

  

Gap detection verifies that no residual deficiency from earlier cycles remains.

  

During the scoping cycle:

  

Structural absence is detected.

  

During the structuring cycle:

  

Boundary misalignment and responsibility overlap are detected.

  

During the drafting cycle:

  

Behavioral drift and abstraction escalation are detected.

  

During the refinement cycle:

  

Softness, unanchored claims, and semantic looseness are detected.

  

During the audit cycle:

  

Terminology instability, role-token violation, and cross-sectional contradiction are detected.

  

Gap detection is cumulative.

  

Earlier instability classes should not reappear in later cycles.

  

If they do, contraction may have been incomplete.

---

### **Probing as Stress Validation**

  

Gap detection verifies completeness.

  

Probing evaluates load-bearing capacity.

  

Probing applies controlled structural stress through:

  

• Assertion inversion

  

• Boundary pressure

  

• Scope scaling

  

• Responsibility reassignment

  

• Assumption removal

  

A structure that withstands probing demonstrates internal coherence under stress.

  

A structure that degrades reveals hidden instability.

  

Probing exposes fragility masked by fluency.

  

Fluency alone does not demonstrate stability.

  

Resistance under stress provides stronger evidence.

---

### **Integration With Tolerance Governance**

  

Gap detection and probing operate relative to declared tolerance.

  

Tolerance contracts sequentially across lifecycle cycles.

  

What is acceptable in early formation is unacceptable during governance verification.

  

Probing evaluates whether instability exceeds current tolerance.

  

If deviation remains within threshold, progression continues.

  

If deviation exceeds threshold, correction is required.

  

This reduces premature tightening and mitigates decay risk.

---

### **Reinjection and Structural Confirmation**

  

After correction, Reinjection tests stability.

  

A unit that can be reintroduced without reopening the same instability has reached convergence for its lifecycle cycle objective.

  

If Reinjection reactivates prior error classes, contraction was incomplete.

  

Gap detection identifies omission.

  

Probing identifies weakness.

  

Reinjection verifies correction.

  

Together, they complete the convergence cycle.
