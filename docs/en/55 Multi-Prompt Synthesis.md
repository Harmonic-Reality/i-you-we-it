## **Multi-Prompt Synthesis**

  

Single-Prompt Synthesis produces bounded units.

  

Sustained architectural development requires constraint propagation across sequential prompts within a continuous working state.

  

Multi-Prompt Synthesis is the persistence of stabilized structural constraints across prompts, enabling full-artifact synthesis pass and layer-level derivation without renegotiating foundational architecture.

  

The mechanism is horizontal constraint propagation.

  

It is not hierarchical elevation.

  

It is not abstraction layering.

  

It is bounded continuity across prompts.

---

### **Structural Preconditions**

  

Multi-Prompt Synthesis requires prior stabilization of:

  

• Spine

• Lens

• Tolerance regime

• Terminology normalization

• Boundary conditions

  

Without upstream stabilization, sequential prompts may amplify drift rather than accumulate structure.

  

Constraint propagation depends on institutional-grade coherence established earlier.

---

### **Prompt-Level Bounded Resolution**

  

Each prompt resolves a bounded scope.

  

Resolution may include:

  

• Refining a section

• Tightening terminology

• Contracting tolerance

• Clarifying boundary conditions

  

The scope remains local.

  

Structural constraints persist across prompts.

  

Because architecture remains declared and stable, subsequent prompts build upon prior stabilization without redefinition.

  

Minimal directional input becomes increasingly sufficient once constraints are stabilized.

  

The prompt no longer reconstructs context.

  

It extends it.

---

### **Drift Accumulation Across Prompts**

  

Constraint persistence is bounded.

  

Across many prompts:

  

• Terminology may soften.

• Boundaries may blur.

• Tolerance may widen implicitly.

• Emphasis may shift.

  

Drift tends to be gradual.

  

It rarely appears as abrupt contradiction.

  

If uncorrected, accumulation may destabilize architecture.

---

### **Periodic Tolerance Reinforcement**

  

Stability requires deliberate reinforcement.

  

Reinforcement may include:

  

• Reasserting terminology

• Revalidating tolerance thresholds

• Restating boundary conditions

• Confirming lens posture

  

Reinforcement compresses accumulated deviation.

  

This converts long prompt sequences into bounded propagation cycles.

  

Constraint persistence becomes more durable when reinforcement is periodic rather than reactive.

---

### **Full-Artifact Synthesis Pass Emergence**

  

When constraint propagation is stable and drift is controlled, larger outcomes become feasible.

  

Entire full-artifact synthesis passes can emerge from incremental prompts.

  

Layer-level synthesis becomes possible without architectural reset.

  

Derivation is an outcome of sequential constraint persistence.

  

The mechanism is not batching.

  

It is prompt-level propagation under stabilized architecture.
